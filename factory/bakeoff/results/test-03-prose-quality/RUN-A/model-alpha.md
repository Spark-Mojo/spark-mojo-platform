# WorkboardMojo User Guide

**Last updated:** April 9, 2026

---

## Introduction

WorkboardMojo is your digital task list inside Spark Mojo. It shows every task that needs your attention at your practice. Things like "call a patient back," "submit a prior authorization," or "review a denied claim."

You can see all tasks, claim new ones, mark them done, reassign them to coworkers, add notes, and keep track of what is urgent. Think of it as a shared whiteboard for your whole office—but digital and always up to date.

---

## Who Uses This

WorkboardMojo is for staff at behavioral health practices who need to track and complete daily tasks. This includes:

- **Therapists** who need to follow up with patients, complete documentation, or get approvals
- **Office managers** who need to assign work, track what is overdue, and keep the office running smoothly
- **Front desk staff** who need to schedule appointments, verify insurance, and return calls
- **Billing coordinators** who need to submit claims, appeal denials, and reconcile payments
- **Clinical support staff** who need to request records, process referrals, and coordinate care

If you have a login to Spark Mojo, you can use WorkboardMojo.

## How to Log In and Find WorkboardMojo

1. Open your web browser. Chrome, Firefox, or Edge all work.
2. Go to your practice's Spark Mojo address. Your office manager will give you this. It looks something like `https://yourpractice.app.sparkmojo.com`.
3. Enter your email address and password on the login page.
4. Click the "Log In" button.
5. You will see the Spark Mojo desktop. It looks like a computer desktop with windows on it.
6. Look for the window titled "My Tasks" or "Workboard." That is WorkboardMojo.
7. If you do not see it, ask your office manager. They may need to add it to your desktop.

## How to Understand Your Task List

When WorkboardMojo opens, you will see several sections on your screen.

### The Stats Bar (Top Section)

At the very top, you will see four colored cards:

- **Active Tasks** — How many tasks are currently open (not completed or canceled). Click this card to see all active tasks.
- **Urgent** — How many tasks are marked as "Urgent" priority. Click this card to see only urgent tasks.
- **Overdue** — How many tasks have passed their due date. Click this card to see only overdue tasks.
- **Waiting** — How many tasks are paused, waiting for something external. Click this card to see only waiting tasks.

Click any stat card to filter the list. Click the same card again to remove the filter.

### The Filter Tabs

Below the stats bar, you will see tabs:

- **All** — Shows every task.
- **Action** — Shows only tasks where you need to do something.
- **Review** — Shows only tasks where you need to check something.
- **Approval** — Shows only tasks that need your approval.

You will also see a "Source" dropdown. This lets you filter tasks by where they came from (Manual, EHR, etc.). And there is a "Show Completed" button that includes tasks already finished.

### The Task List

Below the filter tabs, you will see the list of tasks. Each task is a row with these columns:

- **TASK** — The task's title and ID number (like TASK-00001).
- **TYPE** — A colored badge showing if the task is an Action, Review, Approval, etc.
- **STATUS** — A colored badge showing the current status (New, Ready, In Progress, Waiting, or Blocked).
- **SOURCE** — Where the task came from.
- **DUE DATE** — When the task is due. Overdue tasks show a red "Overdue" label.
- **ASSIGNED** — Who the task is assigned to. You will see their initials and first name. If no one is assigned, you will see a red "Unassigned" badge.
- **ACTIONS** — A "View" button (if assigned) or a "Claim" button (if unassigned).

### The Color Stripe on the Left Edge

Each task row has a thin colored stripe on its left edge. This tells you the priority:

- **Red** = Urgent
- **Orange** = High
- **Teal/green** = Medium
- **Gray** = Low

## How to Open a Task and Read Its Details

1. Find the task you want to look at in the task list.
2. Click anywhere on that task's row.
3. A panel slides in from the right side of the screen. This is the "task detail drawer."
4. In the drawer, you will see:
   - The task title at the top.
   - A badge showing the task type and a dot showing the priority.
   - A "Status" dropdown showing the current status.
   - Details like who the task is assigned to, the due date, the source system, and any completion criteria.
   - A "Comments" section with any notes people have added.
   - A "State History" section (click the arrow to expand) showing every status change.
   - A "Complete Task" button at the bottom.
5. To close the drawer, click the X button in the top-right corner, click anywhere outside the drawer, or press the Escape key.

---

## How to Mark a Task Complete

1. Click on the task you want to complete. The task detail drawer opens.
2. Look at the bottom of the drawer. You will see a large coral-colored button that says "Complete Task."
3. Click the "Complete Task" button.
4. The button changes to say "Completing..." for a moment.
5. The drawer closes automatically.
6. The task disappears from your active task list. It is now marked as "Completed."
7. If you want to see completed tasks later, click the "Show Completed" button in the filter tabs.

When you complete a task, several things happen:

- The task's status changes to "Completed."
- A timestamp is recorded for when the task was completed.
- A state history entry is created showing the transition and who completed it.
- The task disappears from the active list (but it still exists—you can find it by clicking "Show Completed").
- The stats bar updates: the "Active Tasks" count goes down by one.

Completed tasks are never deleted. They are kept forever for records.

## How to Reassign a Task to Someone Else

1. Click on the task you want to reassign. The task detail drawer opens.
2. In the "Details" section, look for a small pencil icon next to the "Details" label. Click the pencil icon.
3. An edit form appears with fields for "Assigned User," "Assigned Role," and "Due Date."
4. To assign to a specific person, start typing their name or email in the "Assigned User" field. You will see suggestions appear. Click on the correct person.
5. To assign to a role (so anyone in that role can claim it), use the "Assigned Role" field.
6. You can also change the due date if needed.
7. Click the "Save" button.
8. The task row updates to show the new assignee.
9. If you change your mind, click "Cancel" to discard your changes.

## How to Add a Comment to a Task

1. Click on the task you want to comment on. The task detail drawer opens.
2. Scroll down to the "Comments" section.
3. At the bottom of the comments section, you will see a text box that says "Add a comment..."
4. Click in the text box and type your comment. For example: "Called patient, left voicemail. Will try again tomorrow."
5. Click the "Send" button (or press Enter).
6. Your comment appears immediately at the top of the comments list. You do not need to reload the page.

---

## How to Change a Task's Due Date

1. Click on the task whose due date you want to change. The task detail drawer opens.
2. Click the pencil icon next to "Details."
3. Find the "Due Date" field.
4. Click on the date field and select a new date from the calendar picker.
5. Click "Save."
6. The task row updates to show the new due date.

---

## How to Change a Task's Status

1. Click on the task whose status you want to change. The task detail drawer opens.
2. Find the "Status" dropdown near the top of the drawer.
3. Click the dropdown and select a new status. The options are:
   - New, Ready, In Progress, Waiting, Blocked, Completed, Canceled, Failed.
4. If you select "Blocked" or "Failed," a text box appears asking for a reason. Type in the reason (for example: "Waiting on insurance response" or "Wrong patient info"). Then click "Confirm."
5. The status updates immediately in both the drawer and the task list.

Here is what each status means:

- **New** — Task was just created. No one has started working on it.
- **Ready** — Task is ready to be worked on. Prerequisites are met.
- **In Progress** — Someone is actively working on this task.
- **Waiting** — Task is paused, waiting for an external event (patient callback, insurance response, etc.).
- **Blocked** — Task cannot proceed. Something is wrong. A reason is required.
- **Completed** — Task is done. Disappears from the active task list.
- **Canceled** — Task was canceled. Disappears from the active task list.
- **Failed** — Task failed. A reason is required. Disappears from the active task list.

## How to Use the Kanban View

The default view is a list (rows of tasks). You can also switch to a Kanban view, which shows tasks as cards organized into columns by status.

1. Look at the top-right area of WorkboardMojo for two buttons: "List" and "Kanban."
2. Click "Kanban."
3. You will see columns labeled: New, Ready, In Progress, Waiting, Blocked, and Other.
4. Each task appears as a card in the column matching its current status.
5. Each column header shows how many tasks are in that column.
6. You can drag a task card from one column to another to change its status. For example, drag a task from "New" to "In Progress" to start working on it.
7. If you drag a task to "Blocked," you will be prompted to enter a reason before the status change is saved.
8. Click any task card to open the task detail drawer, just like in list view.
9. Your view preference (List or Kanban) is remembered. The next time you open WorkboardMojo, it will use the same view you last chose.

## How to Claim a Task from the Role Queue

When tasks are assigned to a role but not to a specific person, they have a pulsing red "Unassigned" badge. This means the task is sitting in a role queue and nobody has claimed it yet.

If you see an unassigned task that falls within your responsibilities:

1. Look for the "Claim" button on the right side of the task row.
2. Click "Claim."
3. You will see a brief spinner, and then the task will be assigned to you. Its status will automatically change to "In Progress" (if it was previously "New" or "Ready").
4. The red badge will be replaced by your initials avatar.

If someone else claims the task at the same time you do, you will see an error message saying "This task was already claimed." This is normal—just move on to the next task.

## How to Create a New Task

1. Look for the "New Task" button in the WorkboardMojo toolbar area (usually a "+" icon or a button labeled "New Task").
2. Click it. A dialog box appears.
3. Fill in the fields:
   - **Title** (required): A short description of what needs to be done. For example: "Call Mrs. Johnson about rescheduling."
   - **Task Type**: Select the type that best fits. "Action" means you need to do something. "Review" means you need to check something. "Approval" means you need to approve something.
   - **Priority**: How urgent is this? Low, Medium, High, or Urgent.
   - **Assigned User**: Who should do this task? Start typing a name to search.
   - **Assigned Role**: Or assign to a role so anyone in that role can claim it.
   - **Due Date**: When should this be done?
   - **Source**: Where did this task come from? Usually "Manual" for tasks you create yourself.
   - **Description**: Optional longer description with more details.
4. Click "Create."
5. The new task appears in your task list immediately.

## How to Sort Your Task List

By default, tasks are sorted by due date (earliest first). But you can sort by other columns too.

1. Click on any column header (like "DUE DATE" or "STATUS") to sort by that column.
2. Click again to reverse the sort order (ascending to descending or vice versa).
3. Your sort preference is saved automatically. The next time you open WorkboardMojo, it will use the same sort order.

---

## How to Filter Tasks by Source

You can filter tasks by where they came from.

1. In the filter bar, click the "Source" dropdown.
2. Select a source (for example, "EHR" or "n8n").
3. The task list updates to show only tasks from that source.
4. To remove the filter, select "All" from the dropdown.

This is useful when you want to focus on a specific category of work, like all the tasks generated by insurance denials or all the tasks from the front desk scheduling system.

## Real-World Use Case Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Monday Morning Triage

It is Monday morning. You arrive at work and open Spark Mojo. You see WorkboardMojo on your desktop.

1. Click on WorkboardMojo to open it.
2. Look at the stats bar at the top. Check the "Overdue" card first.
3. Click the "Overdue" card. You see only the tasks that have passed their due date.
4. Work through these first. Open each one, take the needed action, and mark it complete.
5. When you finish the overdue tasks, click the "Overdue" card again to clear the filter.
6. Click the "Urgent" card to see your urgent tasks. Handle those next.
7. Clear the filter and work through the rest of your list sorted by due date.

### Scenario 2: A Patient Calls to Reschedule

A patient calls and asks to reschedule their appointment. You need to create a follow-up task.

1. Click "New Task" in WorkboardMojo.
2. In the title field, type "Reschedule appointment for Jane Doe—requested 4/15 afternoon."
3. Set the task type to "Action."
4. Set the priority to "Medium."
5. Set the due date to today (since you want to get it done before end of day).
6. Leave the assigned user as yourself.
7. Click "Create." The new task appears in your list.
8. After you reschedule the appointment, open the task and click "Complete Task."

### Scenario 3: A Denied Claim Needs Follow-Up

Your practice receives a denied claim notification. Your billing system creates a task automatically (source system: "n8n" or "EHR").

1. You see a new task appear in your WorkboardMojo list with a type of "Action" and a red priority stripe if it is urgent.
2. Click on the task to open the drawer. You see the details including which claim was denied and why.
3. Add a comment like "Called Aetna, they need additional documentation. Faxing records today."
4. Change the status to "In Progress."
5. When you have faxed the records, update the status to "Waiting" (waiting for Aetna to process).
6. When the claim is resolved, mark the task complete.

### Scenario 4: Handing Off Your Tasks Before Vacation

You are going on vacation next week and need to reassign your tasks.

1. Open WorkboardMojo. Look at your active tasks.
2. For each task, click on it to open the drawer.
3. Click the pencil icon next to "Details."
4. In the "Assigned User" field, type your coworker's name and select them.
5. Click "Save." The task now shows your coworker's name in the "Assigned" column.
6. Repeat for each task.
7. Add a comment to each task saying "Reassigned to [coworker name] for vacation coverage 4/10-4/17."

### Scenario 5: Claiming a Task from the Role Queue

You are a billing coordinator. You see several tasks with red "Billing" badges in the Assigned column—these are unassigned tasks in the billing role queue.

1. Pick the one with the earliest due date.
2. Click the "Claim" button on that task row.
3. You see a brief spinner, and then the task is assigned to you with your initials avatar.
4. The status changes to "In Progress."
5. Open the task drawer to read the details and start working on it.

### Scenario 6: Using the Kanban View for a Busy Day

You have 15 active tasks and want to visualize your workflow.

1. Click the "Kanban" toggle.
2. You see your tasks organized into columns: New, Ready, In Progress, Waiting, and Blocked.
3. You can quickly see how many tasks are in each stage.
4. Drag a task from "New" to "In Progress" to start working on it.
5. When you are done, drag it to the column that no longer shows (completed tasks are removed).
6. This gives you a bird's-eye view of your workload that the list view cannot.

### Scenario 7: Adding Context to a Task for Your Coworker

A coworker asks you about a task that was originally yours.

1. Open the task by clicking on it.
2. Scroll down to the Comments section.
3. Type a detailed note: "I called the insurance company on 4/3. They said the appeal will be reviewed within 10 business days. Reference number: REF-9876."
4. Click "Send." Your coworker can now see this note when they open the same task.

### Scenario 8: Checking What You Completed Last Week

Your manager asks what you accomplished last week.

1. In WorkboardMojo, click the "Show Completed" toggle in the filter bar.
2. You see all completed tasks appear in the list with a strikethrough on their titles and a faded appearance.
3. These are sorted by their due date.
4. You can click on any completed task to see the full details including who completed it, when, and all comments.

### Scenario 9: A Task Is Blocked and You Need Help

You are working on a task but cannot proceed because you need information from another department.

1. Open the task drawer.
2. Change the Status dropdown from "In Progress" to "Blocked."
3. A text box appears asking for a reason. Type: "Need patient authorization number from Clinical team—emailed Dr. Smith on 4/5."
4. Click "Confirm." The task now shows a "Blocked" status badge.
5. Your manager can see this and help unblock you.

### Scenario 10: End-of-Day Review

At the end of your work day, open WorkboardMojo for a quick review.

1. Look at the stats bar. Note how many active tasks you have.
2. Click the "Overdue" card—if the number is zero, great. If there are overdue tasks, see if any can be completed quickly.
3. For tasks you cannot finish today, add a comment explaining where you left off.
4. This way, if someone else needs to pick up the task, they know the current state.

### Scenario 11: Filtering Tasks by Source

You want to see only tasks that came from the EHR system.

1. In the filter bar, click the "Source" dropdown.
2. Select "EHR." You see the task list update to show only EHR-sourced tasks.
3. This is useful when you want to focus on a specific category of work, like all the tasks generated by insurance denials or all the tasks from the front desk scheduling system.

### Scenario 12: Sorting Your Task List

By default, tasks are sorted by due date (earliest first). But you can sort by other columns too.

1. Click the "TYPE" column header to sort by task type.
2. Click "STATUS" to sort by status.
3. Click "TASK" to sort alphabetically by title.
4. Click any column header twice to reverse the sort order.
5. Your sort preference is saved automatically—the next time you open WorkboardMojo, it will use the same sort order.

## Glossary

**Urgency** — How important or time-sensitive a task is. Tasks can be Low, Medium, High, or Urgent priority. Urgent tasks have a red priority stripe and are counted in the "Urgent" stat card.

**Role Queue** — A pool of tasks assigned to a role (like "Billing" or "Front Desk") but not to a specific person. Any staff member who has that role can "claim" a task from the role queue to take ownership of it.

**Unclaimed / Unowned** — A task that has a role assigned but no specific person assigned. These tasks show a red badge and a pulsing animation to draw attention. Someone needs to claim them.

**Kanban** — A visual way of organizing tasks into columns based on their status. Each column represents a status (New, Ready, In Progress, Waiting, Blocked). You can drag tasks between columns.

**Drawer** — The panel that slides in from the right side of the screen when you click on a task. It shows the full details of that task.

**Canonical State / Status** — The official status of a task in the system. The possible statuses are: New, Ready, In Progress, Waiting, Blocked, Completed, Canceled, and Failed.

**Source System** — Where a task came from. "Manual" means a person created it by hand. Other sources like "EHR," "n8n," or "Stripe" mean the task was created automatically by another system.

**SLA** — Service Level Agreement. The number of hours a task should be completed within. If a task exceeds its SLA, it is considered "breached." (SLA tracking is planned but not yet fully active.)

**Completion Criteria** — A description of what "done" looks like for a task. This helps you know exactly what is expected before you can mark the task complete.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Where did my task go? I had a task and now it is gone.**
If you completed a task or someone else completed it, it will not show in the default view. Click "Show Completed" in the filter bar to see finished tasks.

**2. Why does a task have a red pulsing badge?**
That task is assigned to a role but not to a specific person. It needs someone to claim it.

**3. What does "Overdue" mean?**
It means the task's due date has already passed and the task is not yet completed.

**4. Can I undo completing a task?**
Not from WorkboardMojo directly. Ask your office manager or Spark Mojo admin to change the status back in the admin system.

**5. How do I know who completed a task?**
Open the completed task. Look at the State History section (click the arrow to expand it). You will see who changed the status to "Completed" and when.

**6. Can two people work on the same task at the same time?**
The task can only be assigned to one person at a time. If you need to collaborate, use the comments section to communicate.

**7. What happens if I try to claim a task someone else already claimed?**
You will see an error message saying "This task was already claimed." This just means someone else got to it first. Move on to the next available task.

**8. Why is my task showing "Blocked"?**
Someone changed the status to "Blocked" because something is preventing the task from being completed. Look at the status reason in the task details to understand what the blocker is.

**9. Can I create a task for someone else?**
Yes. When creating a new task, fill in the "Assigned User" field with their name. The task will appear in their task list.

**10. How do I see tasks for the whole office, not just mine?**
WorkboardMojo currently shows all tasks for the entire practice. Everyone sees the same list.

**11. Can I print my task list?**
There is no built-in print feature yet. You can use your browser's print function (Ctrl+P or Cmd+P) as a workaround.

**12. Why does the stats bar show a different number than what I count in the list?**
If you have a filter active (like a task type tab or source filter), the list may be filtered but the stats bar counts all tasks. Clear your filters to see the full list.

**13. What is the difference between "New" and "Ready"?**
"New" means the task was just created and nobody has looked at it yet. "Ready" means the task has been reviewed and is ready to be worked on.

**14. How do I delete a task?**
You cannot delete tasks from WorkboardMojo. You can change the status to "Canceled" to remove it from the active list.

**15. Can I set a reminder for a task?**
Reminders are not available yet. For now, check your overdue and due-today tasks each morning.

**16. What does "Waiting" mean?**
It means the task is paused because something external needs to happen first—like waiting for a patient callback or an insurance response.

**17. How far back can I see completed tasks?**
All completed tasks are kept forever. Click "Show Completed" to see them.

**18. Can I reopen a completed task?**
Not from WorkboardMojo directly. Ask your admin to change the status back to a non-completed state.

**19. What if I accidentally claim the wrong task?**
Open the task drawer, click the pencil icon, clear your name from the "Assigned User" field (or assign it to someone else), and click Save.

**20. Why do some tasks have a colored stripe on the left and others do not?**
Every task has a stripe. Red means Urgent, orange means High, teal/green means Medium, and gray means Low. Completed tasks always show a gray stripe.

**21. What does the small badge next to a task title mean (like "Action" or "Review")?**
It shows the task type—what kind of work is needed. "Action" means do something. "Review" means check something. "Approval" means approve something.

**22. Why do I see tasks that are not assigned to me?**
WorkboardMojo currently shows all tasks for the entire practice. This is by design for small offices so everyone can see the full picture.

**23. Can I drag tasks between columns in Kanban view?**
Yes. Click and hold on a task card, then drag it to a different column to change its status. If you drag to "Blocked," you will need to enter a reason.

**24. What is the difference between the List view and Kanban view?**
The List view shows tasks as rows in a table. The Kanban view shows tasks as cards organized into columns by status. Both show the same tasks—just organized differently.

**25. How do I switch between List and Kanban view?**
Look for the "List" and "Kanban" buttons near the top-right of WorkboardMojo. Click the one you want.
