# WorkboardMojo User Guide

**Last updated:** April 2026
**Who this is for:** Billing coordinators, front desk staff, office managers, and clinical support staff

---

## Introduction

WorkboardMojo is your task list inside Spark Mojo. It replaces sticky notes, scribbled lists, and "did you remember to...?" emails with a shared digital board everyone on your team can see.

When something needs to get done — a patient to call back, a claim to follow up on, a document to review — it becomes a task here. You can create tasks, claim ones that belong to your team, mark them done, leave notes for your coworkers, and see at a glance what is urgent.

That is the whole idea: one place to see what needs doing, who is doing it, and when it is due.

---

## Who Uses This?

WorkboardMojo is for anyone at your practice who handles tasks.

- **Billing coordinators** track claim denials, follow-ups, and payment issues.
- **Front desk staff** manage appointment changes, patient reminders, and intake paperwork.
- **Practice managers** keep an eye on what is moving and what is blocked.
- **Clinical support staff** handle clinical tasks, referrals, and records requests.

If you have work that needs to get done, tracked, and completed — this is for you.

## How to Log In

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Edge all work fine).
2. Go to your practice's Spark Mojo address. It will look something like `https://yourpractice.app.sparkmojo.com`. Your office manager can give you the exact link.
3. Enter your email and password, then click **Log In**.
4. You will see the Spark Mojo desktop — a screen with windows on it, like your computer desktop at home.
5. Find the window called **My Tasks** or **Workboard**. Click on it to open WorkboardMojo.
6. If you do not see it, ask your office manager. They may need to add it to your desktop layout.

## What You Will See

### The Stats Bar

At the top of WorkboardMojo, there are four cards with numbers:

| Card | What it means |
|------|---------------|
| **Active Tasks** | How many tasks are currently open and waiting to be done. |
| **Urgent** | How many tasks are marked "Urgent" priority. These need attention first. |
| **Overdue** | How many tasks have passed their due date without being completed. |
| **Waiting** | How many tasks are paused, waiting on something outside your control. |

Click any card to filter the list below it. Click it again to remove the filter.

### The Filter Tabs

Below the stats bar, there are tabs for filtering by task type:

- **All** — shows every task.
- **Action** — tasks where you need to do something (make a call, send a form, etc.).
- **Review** — tasks where you need to check or verify something.
- **Approval** — tasks that need your sign-off.

There is also a **Source** dropdown on the right. Use this to filter tasks by where they came from (like "Manual" for tasks you typed in, or "EHR" for tasks that came from your electronic health records system).

If you want to see tasks you have already finished, click **Show Completed**.

### The Task List

The main part of the screen shows your tasks as rows in a table. Each row has:

- **Task** — the title of the task and its ID (like TASK-00001).
- **Type** — a colored badge showing what kind of task it is (Action, Review, Approval, etc.).
- **Status** — a badge showing where the task is in its lifecycle (New, In Progress, Waiting, etc.).
- **Source** — where the task came from.
- **Due Date** — when it is due. You might see "Today," "Tomorrow," or the actual date.
- **Assigned** — who the task belongs to. You will see their initials in a circle. If no one is assigned yet, you will see a red **Unassigned** badge.
- **Actions** — a **View** button to open the task, or a **Claim** button if the task is waiting for someone to pick it up.

### The Priority Stripe

On the far left of each task row, there is a thin colored stripe. This tells you the priority at a glance:

- **Red stripe** = Urgent
- **Orange stripe** = High priority
- **Teal/green stripe** = Medium priority
- **Gray stripe** = Low priority

## How to Open a Task

1. Find the task you want to look at in the list.
2. Click anywhere on that row.
3. A panel slides in from the right side of the screen. This is called the **drawer**.
4. The drawer shows:
   - The task title
   - Its type badge and priority dot
   - A status dropdown you can change
   - Details: who it is assigned to, the due date, the source, and any description
   - A comments section where people have left notes
   - A history section showing every status change (click the arrow to expand it)
   - A **Complete Task** button at the bottom
5. To close the drawer, click the X in the top-right corner, click outside the drawer, or press Escape on your keyboard.

---

## How to Mark a Task Complete

1. Click on the task to open the drawer.
2. Click the **Complete Task** button at the bottom. It is a large coral-colored button.
3. The button will briefly say "Completing..."
4. The drawer closes automatically.
5. The task disappears from your active list. It is now marked as "Completed."
6. To see it again later, click **Show Completed** in the filter bar.

---

## How to Claim an Unassigned Task

When a task has a red **Unassigned** badge, it is sitting in a team queue waiting for someone to take it.

1. Click **Claim** on the right side of the task row.
2. You will see a brief spinner while the system updates.
3. The task is now assigned to you. The red badge disappears and your initials appear instead.
4. The status changes to "In Progress" if it was previously "New" or "Ready."

If two people try to claim the same task at the exact same time, one of you will see an error message: "This task was already claimed." This just means your coworker was a few seconds faster. Move on to the next unassigned task.

---

## How to Reassign a Task to Someone Else

1. Open the task by clicking on it.
2. Click the pencil icon next to **Details** in the drawer.
3. An edit form appears with fields for **Assigned User**, **Assigned Role**, and **Due Date**.
4. To assign to a specific person, type their name in the **Assigned User** field. A dropdown will appear with suggestions — click the right person.
5. To assign to a role (so anyone on that team can pick it up), use the **Assigned Role** field instead.
6. Change the due date if needed.
7. Click **Save**. The task row updates immediately to show the new assignee.

To cancel your changes, click **Cancel** instead of Save.

---

## How to Add a Comment

1. Open the task.
2. Scroll down to the **Comments** section.
3. Click the text box that says "Add a comment..."
4. Type your note. For example: "Called the patient, no answer. Will try again tomorrow."
5. Click **Send** or press Enter.
6. Your comment appears immediately at the top of the comments list. No need to reload the page.

---

## How to Change a Task's Status

1. Open the task.
2. Find the **Status** dropdown near the top of the drawer.
3. Click it and pick a new status. Your options are:
   - **New** — task was just created
   - **Ready** — task is ready to be worked on
   - **In Progress** — you are actively working on it
   - **Waiting** — task is paused, waiting on something external
   - **Blocked** — something is preventing progress
   - **Completed** — task is done
   - **Canceled** — task was canceled
   - **Failed** — task could not be completed
4. If you pick **Blocked** or **Failed**, a text box appears asking for a reason. Type what is blocking the task. Then click **Confirm**.
5. The status updates right away in both the drawer and the task list.

---

## How to Change a Due Date

1. Open the task.
2. Click the pencil icon next to **Details**.
3. Click the **Due Date** field and pick a new date from the calendar.
4. Click **Save**.

---

## How to Create a New Task

1. Click the **New Task** button or the **+** icon in the toolbar.
2. A dialog box appears. Fill in what you know:
   - **Title** (required) — a short description of what needs to happen. Example: "Reschedule appointment for Jane Doe."
   - **Task Type** — what kind of work is this? "Action" means do something. "Review" means check something. "Approval" means sign off on something.
   - **Priority** — how urgent is it? Low, Medium, High, or Urgent.
   - **Assigned User** — who should handle this? Start typing their name.
   - **Assigned Role** — or assign it to a team (like "Billing") so anyone on that team can claim it.
   - **Due Date** — when should this be done?
   - **Source** — where did this task come from? Usually "Manual" for tasks you create yourself.
   - **Description** — optional longer notes with more detail.
3. Click **Create**.
4. The new task appears in your list right away.

## List View vs. Kanban View

WorkboardMojo has two ways to display your tasks.

### List View

This is the default. Tasks appear as rows in a table. This is good when you want to see a lot of tasks at once and sort through them quickly.

### Kanban View

Click the **Kanban** button in the top-right area to switch. Tasks appear as cards organized into columns by status:

- **New**
- **Ready**
- **In Progress**
- **Waiting**
- **Blocked**
- **Other**

Each column shows how many tasks are in it. You can drag a card from one column to another to change its status. If you drag a card to "Blocked," the system asks you to enter a reason first.

Click any card to open its drawer, just like in list view.

Your view preference is saved. The next time you open WorkboardMojo, it will remember whether you preferred List or Kanban.

---

## How to Sort Your Tasks

Click any column header to sort by that column. Click again to reverse the sort order (earliest to latest, or vice versa). Your sort choice is remembered between sessions.

Common sort options:

- **Due Date** — see what is coming up soonest
- **Priority** — see the most urgent tasks first
- **Status** — group tasks by where they are in the workflow
- **Title** — alphabetical order

## Real-World Use Cases

### 1. Monday Morning Check-In

It is Monday morning. You open WorkboardMojo and see the stats bar at the top.

First, click **Overdue**. Handle anything that slipped through over the weekend. Open each one, take action, and mark it complete.

When the overdue list is clear, click **Overdue** again to remove that filter. Then click **Urgent** to see what needs your immediate attention today.

Work through those, then clear the filter and tackle the rest of your list in due-date order.

### 2. Patient Calls to Reschedule

A patient calls and asks to move their Tuesday appointment to Thursday afternoon.

1. Click **New Task**.
2. Title: "Reschedule appointment for Maria Santos — requested Thursday afternoon."
3. Type: Action.
4. Priority: Medium.
5. Due Date: Today (you want to get this done before end of day).
6. Assigned User: leave as yourself, or assign to whoever handles scheduling.
7. Click **Create**.
8. Work on the task, make the change in your scheduling system, then open the task and click **Complete Task**.

### 3. Follow-Up on a Denied Insurance Claim

Your billing system creates a task automatically when a claim is denied. You see it appear in WorkboardMojo with a source of "n8n" or "EHR."

1. Click on the task to open the drawer.
2. Read the details — it will say which claim was denied and why.
3. Add a comment: "Called Aetna, they need additional documentation. Faxing records today."
4. Change the status to **In Progress**.
5. When you have faxed the records, change the status to **Waiting** (you are now waiting on Aetna to process).
6. When the claim is resolved, click **Complete Task**.

### 4. Reassigning Tasks Before Vacation

You are leaving for vacation on Friday. You need to hand off your open tasks to a coworker.

1. Open WorkboardMojo and look at your active tasks.
2. For each task, open the drawer, click the pencil icon, and type your coworker's name in the **Assigned User** field.
3. Click **Save**.
4. Add a comment to each task: "Reassigned to Jordan for vacation coverage 4/10–4/17."
5. Repeat for all open tasks.

Your coworker will see these tasks assigned to them and will have your notes to pick up where you left off.

### 5. Picking Up Work from the Billing Queue

You are a billing coordinator. You notice several tasks with red **Billing** badges — these are unassigned tasks sitting in the billing role queue.

1. Look for the task with the earliest due date.
2. Click **Claim** on that task.
3. The task is now yours. Open the drawer and start working on it.

### 6. Using Kanban to See Your Workflow at a Glance

You have 12 active tasks and want to see where everything stands.

1. Click **Kanban** in the top-right.
2. You see columns: New, Ready, In Progress, Waiting, Blocked.
3. You can quickly see that you have 4 tasks in New, 2 in In Progress, and 1 in Waiting.
4. Drag a task from New to In Progress as you start working on it.
5. Drag it again to Waiting when you are waiting on a response.
6. Completed tasks disappear from the board.

This gives you a visual sense of your workload that the list view cannot.

### 7. Leaving Notes for a Coworker

A task was originally yours but is now assigned to a coworker. You have information they need.

1. Open the task.
2. Scroll to **Comments**.
3. Type: "I called the insurance company on 4/3. They said the appeal will be reviewed within 10 business days. Reference number: REF-9876."
4. Click **Send**.

Your coworker opens the task and sees your note right away.

### 8. Reviewing What You Finished Last Week

Your manager asks what you accomplished last week.

1. In WorkboardMojo, click **Show Completed**.
2. You see all finished tasks with strikethrough titles.
3. Click on any completed task to see when it was finished and who completed it.
4. Use this to give your manager a summary.

### 9. A Task Gets Blocked — What Now?

You are working on a task but cannot finish it because you need something from another department.

1. Open the task.
2. Change the status to **Blocked**.
3. A text box appears. Type: "Need patient authorization number from Clinical team — emailed Dr. Smith on 4/5, waiting for response."
4. Click **Confirm**.

Your manager can see the task is blocked and reach out to help unstick it.

### 10. End-of-Day Review

Before you log off for the day, spend two minutes in WorkboardMojo.

1. Look at the stats bar. If **Overdue** shows a number, see if you can knock out one or two before leaving.
2. For tasks you did not finish, open each one and add a comment: "Left off here: called patient, no answer. Will try again tomorrow."
3. This way, if someone else needs to step in, they know exactly where things stand.

### 11. Focusing on EHR Tasks Only

Your manager asks you to clear all the tasks that came from your EHR system.

1. In the filter bar, click the **Source** dropdown.
2. Select **EHR**.
3. The list now shows only tasks that originated in your electronic health records system.
4. Work through them.
5. When done, change the Source filter back to "All" to see everything again.

### 12. Someone Else Completed Your Task

You created a task and assigned it to a coworker. A few days later, you notice it is gone from the list.

Click **Show Completed**. You will see the task with a strikethrough. Open it and check the State History to see who completed it and when.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Where did my task go?

If you completed a task, it disappears from the main list. Click **Show Completed** to see finished tasks.

### Why is there a red pulsing badge on some tasks?

That task is assigned to a team (like "Billing") but not to a specific person. Someone needs to claim it.

### What does "Overdue" mean?

The due date has passed and the task is not yet completed. Prioritize these.

### Can I undo completing a task?

Not directly in WorkboardMojo. Ask your office manager or Spark Mojo admin to change the status back in the admin system.

### How do I see who completed a task?

Open the task, then expand the **State History** section (click the arrow). You will see who changed the status to "Completed" and when.

### Can two people work on the same task at the same time?

No. A task can only be assigned to one person at a time. Use the comments section to communicate with each other about the task.

### What happens if I try to claim a task someone else already claimed?

You will see an error: "This task was already claimed." Move on to the next available task.

### Why does a task show "Blocked"?

Someone changed its status to Blocked because something is preventing it from moving forward. Check the status reason in the task details to see what the blocker is.

### Can I create a task for someone else?

Yes. When creating a new task, fill in the **Assigned User** field with their name. The task will appear in their task list.

### Can I see tasks for the whole office, not just mine?

Yes. WorkboardMojo shows all tasks for the practice, not just your own. Everyone can see the full picture.

### How do I print my task list?

There is no print button yet. You can use your browser's print function (Ctrl+P or Cmd+P) as a workaround.

### The stats bar shows a different number than what I count in the list.

If you have a filter active (like a type tab or source filter), the list may be filtered while the stats bar counts all tasks. Clear your filters to match the stats.

### What is the difference between "New" and "Ready"?

"New" means the task was just created and no one has looked at it yet. "Ready" means it has been reviewed and is ready to work on.

### How do I delete a task?

You cannot delete tasks from WorkboardMojo. To remove a task from your active list, change its status to "Canceled."

### Can I set a reminder for a task?

Not yet. For now, check your overdue and due-today tasks each morning when you start your day.

### What does "Waiting" mean?

The task is paused. You are waiting on something outside your control — a patient to call back, an insurance company to respond, a coworker to send information.

### How far back can I see completed tasks?

All completed tasks are kept forever. Click **Show Completed** to see them.

### Can I reopen a completed task?

Not directly in WorkboardMojo. Ask your admin to change the status back to a non-completed state.

### I claimed the wrong task by accident. How do I fix it?

Open the task, click the pencil icon, clear your name from the **Assigned User** field (or type someone else's name), and click **Save**.

### Why do some tasks have a colored stripe on the left?

Every task has a stripe showing its priority. Red = Urgent, orange = High, teal/green = Medium, gray = Low.

### What do the colored badges mean next to the task title?

Those are task types. "Action" means do something. "Review" means check something. "Approval" means approve something. The color tells you the type at a glance.

### Can I change who created a task?

No. The **Created By** field is set automatically and cannot be changed.

### What if I need to change the task type or source system?

Ask your office manager or Spark Mojo admin. These fields are set when the task is created and may require special access to change.

## Glossary

**Active task** — A task that is still open and not yet completed or canceled.

**Assigned role** — A team name (like "Billing" or "Front Desk") that a task is assigned to. Anyone on that team can claim it.

**Assigned user** — A specific person that a task is assigned to.

**Canonical state** — The official status of a task in the system. See "Status."

**Claim** — To take ownership of an unassigned task from a role queue.

**Completed task** — A task that has been finished. It disappears from the active list but is kept for records.

**Drawer** — The panel that slides in from the right when you click on a task. It shows the task's full details.

**Kanban** — A visual view of tasks organized into columns by status. You can drag cards between columns to change their status.

**Overdue** — A task whose due date has passed without being completed.

**Priority** — How urgent a task is. Options are Low, Medium, High, and Urgent.

**Role queue** — A collection of tasks assigned to a team but not to a specific person. Team members claim tasks from the queue.

**Source** — Where a task came from. "Manual" means a person typed it in. Other sources like "EHR" or "n8n" mean another system created the task automatically.

**Status** — Where a task is in its lifecycle. Options: New, Ready, In Progress, Waiting, Blocked, Completed, Canceled, Failed.

**Unassigned** — A task that has a role but no specific person assigned to it. It shows a red badge and needs someone to claim it.

**Urgent** — The highest priority level. Tasks with urgent priority have a red stripe on the left edge.
