## Section 1: Executive Summary

**model-beta** is the clear overall winner with the highest total score (129.0/130) and unprecedented consistency (mean range 0.7 across all 6 tests — effectively zero variance). It achieved perfect 25.0 on T01, T02, T03, and T05 with zero range, making it the only model with multiple perfect-consistency tests. **model-delta** is a strong second (126.3) with perfect scores on T03 and T05, but its T01 and T02 scores suffered from output truncation issues requiring retries. **model-alpha** is third (116.7) with the best T04 score (tied with delta at 24.7) but catastrophic T02 variance (range 12 — sometimes produces code instead of specs). The remaining three models show significant weaknesses: **model-epsilon** (107.3) consistently over-scopes and under-decomposes, **model-gamma** (105.7) produced the wrong deliverable entirely on T02 Run A and has FastAPI convention issues, and **model-zeta** (102.7) has the worst consistency (mean range 5.2) with a fundamental architecture violation on T04 Run B. T06 produced zero differentiation — all models scored 5/5 on all runs — indicating the adversarial review test needs harder violations. The key insight: the top two models (beta and delta) are separated by consistency more than capability.
