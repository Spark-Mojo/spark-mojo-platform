## The Arc

### Beat 1 — Empathy + Hard Truth
*Goal: Establish credibility by naming their pain accurately, then reframe it.*

**Slide title:** "You're not behind because you're not working hard enough."

**Talking points:**
You've been fighting fires. We get it — when something is on fire, you put it out.
But fire-fighting is self-perpetuating. Every fire you fight without fixing the
underlying cause starts two more. The reason you feel like you can never get ahead
is not a capacity problem. It is an information and structure problem.

The way you've been approaching it — reactive, unstructured, everyone stepping up
because someone has to — is actively making it harder. Not because anyone is doing
anything wrong. Because without structure, good people spin.

This is not a criticism. This is a diagnosis. And today we're going to show you
what the treatment looks like.

**Tone:** Direct, empathetic, authoritative. Set low expectations for what's coming.

### Beat 2 — The Standard
*Goal: Introduce what good looks like, make them feel the gap, name the people
being failed by the current approach.*

**Slide title:** "Here's what good process management actually requires."

**Talking points:**

Good operational management has four non-negotiables:
1. **Facilitated BPM sessions** with definitive, documented outcomes — not
   stream-of-consciousness calls
2. **A clear RACI** — every workflow has one person Responsible, one Accountable,
   defined Consulted and Informed. Not "everyone."
3. **An SOP library** that is maintained, version-controlled, and non-negotiable
4. **An auditable plan** with owners and deadlines — not a to-do list, a commitment

Here's what we observed: your four-hour call was a genuine attempt at business
process management without the methodology. The signal was in there. But extracting
it took us an enormous amount of time before the real work could begin. With proper
facilitation, the same output would have taken 10 minutes to capture.

The harder truth: Lisa, Sarah, and Kaila are stepping up on process and governance
because they know it needs to happen and nobody else is doing it. But process
management is a specialist skill. You are creating expectations of people who were
not hired for this, and they are doing their best in a vacuum.

That is doing them a disservice. It is doing you a disservice. The person who needs
to stop this pattern and define the structure is you, Erin. Not because it's your
fault — because you're the only one with the authority to fix it.

**Tone:** Sharp but not harsh. Land it, acknowledge it, move on.

### Beat 4 — The Vision + The Price Tag
*Goal: Paint the full picture. Then let the cost land. Then let them feel it.*

**Slide title:** "Solving this properly requires a real solution."

**Talking points:**
What you actually need is a comprehensive, extensible operational platform. Not a
better spreadsheet. Not a workaround. Something that gives you:

- Transparent, collaborative workflows — coverage when someone is out, no more
  "I thought you handled that"
- Auditable task history — every action logged, every decision traceable
- An automation layer that handles the work nobody should be doing manually
- Reduced risk when staff turn over — the knowledge lives in the system, not
  in someone's head
- A platform that grows with you — onboarding today, billing tomorrow, voicemail
  triage, AR recovery, reporting

**Then the pricing slide — build it as a table with three rows:**

---

**Slide: "Here's what this costs if you go to market for it."**

| Option | What you get | Cost |
|--------|-------------|------|
| Process consultant | Design + documentation only. No software. BPM sessions, RACI, SOP library, auditable plan. | $150–200/hr × 200–300 hrs = **$30,000–$60,000** |
| Custom software development | The platform built from scratch by a dev firm. 6–9 months. | **$60,000–$120,000** + $2,000–$4,000/mo maintenance |
| Vendor platform (Kareo, Jane App, etc.) | Off-the-shelf. Doesn't fit your workflows. Requires months of configuration. | $5,000–$15,000 setup + **$100–$500 per provider per month** |

*With 15 providers at $300/provider/month = $54,000/year. Forever.*

**Pause. Let it sit. Let them do the math.**

"And none of those options include the process consulting AND the software AND
the automation layer. You'd be buying all three separately."

**Tone:** Matter-of-fact. Not apologetic. Let them have their reaction.

### Beat 6 — The Demo + The Callback
*Goal: Walk the platform, then land the callback that ties back to Beat 3.*

**Demo flow:**
1. **Queue view** — active onboarding clients, KPI strip, urgency flagging,
   color-coded status. "This is what Sarah sees every morning instead of
   opening a spreadsheet."
2. **Kanban** — drag a card between columns. "This is how you move a client
   through the workflow. Drag. Done. Logged."
3. **Client card** — open one client. Walk the checklist. Check something off
   and show the progress bar move. Log an outreach attempt. "Every action is
   timestamped. Every staff member's initials are on it. No more guessing."
4. **My Tasks** — "This is what a staff member sees when they log in. Their
   queue. Their clients. Their overdue items. Color-coded."
5. **Revenue Cycle module** — show the billing dashboard. Outstanding balances,
   aging buckets, click-to-send reminder. "This is the billing visibility you've
   never had."

require your input before we can move forward?"

prioritized. The loop closes.
